Unique tastes experimental spirit

You know we love hops. Hops with big juicy fruity aromas. The tropical, citrus and stone fruit notes that make our IPAs shine. But we also have serious love for the more subtle, elegant hop varieties. The ones that layer floral, herbal and earthy notes into our lagers, that refine and add nuance to our pilsners. Locher-Hopfen is a family owned farm in Tettnang, in the state of Bavaria, from which we source all of our German grown hops. They not only harvest all the traditional German hop varieties, but also several rare heirloom varieties of various European heritage not grown anywhere else. We love experimenting with these special hops, and when we imagined a vibrant, fresh pilsner to celebrate the coming of spring this year, one in particular felt perfect for the occasion. Petit Blanc is an old, nearly lost French variety, formerly grown in the “Champagne-Ardenne” region, close to Burgundy. Noble European in nature with an intensively fruity and diverse character, with notes of papaya and melon, cherry, lilac, pina colada, lemon blossom, green tea and beeswax. Its complexity reminds us of the complexity of spring; the transition from the cold of winter to the warmth of summer, the variable weather of sudden rain showers and gray clouds followed by blue skies and blinding yellow sun, the aroma of fresh blooming flowers and the raw damp soil from which they emerge. We have as much anticipation for that first sip as for that first spring sun.
